The Scandal Uncovered

A six-month investigation by The Observer has uncovered a massive corruption scheme within the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) involving ‚Ç±2.5 billion in fraudulent road construction contracts across 15 provinces in the Philippines.

Internal documents obtained through whistleblowers reveal a systematic pattern of inflated project costs, substandard materials, and phantom contractors who exist only on paper but received hundreds of millions in government funds.

The scheme involved creating fake construction companies, submitting fraudulent bids, and delivering projects that either don't exist or fall far below safety standards, putting Filipino motorists at risk while enriching corrupt officials and their accomplices.
